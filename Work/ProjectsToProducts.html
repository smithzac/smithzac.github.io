Projects to Products

  1. Announce far and wide that there will be an overarching tech strategy and that it will be published and accessible to all
    1. Continuously update this and share progress with the whole tech department
    2. Otherwise people will continue to go off on their own doing unaligned shit
    3. This is Technology, our Primary Operating Model, our strategy. This serves as an accessible reference to anyone in the business who needs it.
  2. Clarify existing roles and responsibilities and publish
    1. Companies will usually have existing roles and responsibilities
    2. Make sure everyone knows where they stand
  3. Define and agree areas of ownership, purpose, and responsibility
    1. Most commonly this is done via 'Products', but could also be product groups, tech stacks or disciplines, or company objectives
    2. Requires tech and products collaboration. What makes sense, given underlying infrastructure, codebase, repos, but will also be clear to stakeholders within the business?
    3. A mixture of 'stream-aligned' and 'platform' teams may be required.
    4. This step is crucial to removing the incredibly inefficient, bottlenecked, and non-scalable top down management of all work by senior managers
    5. This is also a crucial setup step for connecting people in the business directly with people in tech
  4. Build teams around these Products
    1. Build the teams (or better yet, let them build themselves) with the roles and skills they need to deliver for that area
    2. Let the teams own the Product code and become familiar with it. No need for 'jack of all trades teams can work on anything the business might throw at them'
    3. Let the teams build relationships with their stakeholders and users
  5. Give teams the raw, unfiltered, business objectives and help them understand the business problems to solve, then trust them use their skills and expertise
    1. No centralised management of all tech work (this is not scalable or efficient!)
    2. Build a roadmap of potential outcomes based on business objectives
    3. Let the teams build the product specific backlogs based on potential outcomes from the roadmap
    4. Monitor feedback, measures, and evidence to determine success
  6. Let teams experiment and innovate on their own product, as well as solving business objectives and problems
    1. This is where the real value starts to come in, and is what separates the leading companies like Google and Amazon from the rest
    2. This is the realisation that in trying to hire the best tech people for your company, you're hiring creative and brilliant people, and if given the right environment with a bit of time and space, they can accelerate the business in ways most other companies never even realise is possible.
    3. There will still always be a balance between central company objectives to work on, and team determined product objectives
